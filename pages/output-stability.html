Output-Stability Analysis

Mask, boundary, component, and centroid drift

The v15.2 extension records whether segmentation outputs remain stable when precision, runtime, hardware, threshold, or domain conditions change.

Mask area drift

Absolute and relative foreground-area difference between target output and FP32 reference output.

Boundary IoU drift

Boundary-band overlap against the reference boundary, used to spot erosion, dilation, and edge instability.

Connected component delta

Change in foreground component count, including split, merge, deletion, and target-only noise.

Centroid shift

Pixel and normalized shift in the foreground center of mass. Undefined cases must be recorded explicitly.

False-positive region

Target-only foreground area under the selected reference/target comparison rule.

False-negative region

Reference-only foreground area under the selected reference/target comparison rule.

Required metadata

Every row should include case ID, precision mode, model format, runtime backend, execution provider, hardware lane, threshold policy, calibration policy when relevant, fallback status, and log availability.
